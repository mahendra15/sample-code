About Nagpur

Nagpur is a Tier 3 city along with Chandigarh and Indore where Real estate markets are growing and are perceived to have substantial potential demand. Nagpur is often referred to as the 'Orange City' of India because of its high production of oranges and is situated in the heart of the country. Sitting on the banks of River Nag, the winter capital of Maharashtra experiences mild winters and hot summers. Home to one of the oldest universities in the country mainly specializing in IT, engineering and medicines which is why the city is so attractive to multi nationals looking to expand their operations in the city.

Nagpur is centrally located in India making it an increasingly important logistical hub connecting it to all the prime cities in the country like Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata and Chennai. Historically and culturally rich, the city takes pride in being closely associated with Mahatma Gandhi and Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar and has maintained its traditional values which co- exist alongside the modern Nagpur. Colourful cultural events like tribal dances, handicrafts exhibition and equally famous festivals like Kalidas Mahostav, the Ganesh Utsav and a whole lot more make it an interesting place for artistically inclined souls.

Weather and Climate in Nagpur

As it is located at the centre of the Indian peninsula far from the Bay of Bengal and the Arabian Sea, Nagpur has a tropical wet and dry climate with dry conditions prevailing for most of the year. It receives an annual rainfall of 1,205 mm (47.44 in) from monsoon rains during June to September. Summers are extremely hot lasting from March to June. Winter lasts from November to January, during which temperatures can drop below 10 °C (50 °F).

(Source: World Weather Information Service)
